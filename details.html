Autonomous Robotic System for Precise Underground Water Leak Detection

Abstract

This project introduces a smart, low-cost robotic system designed to detect and accurately localize water leaks in underground main pipelines and distribution networks using a combination of pressure sensing, ultrasonic measurement, IMU-based tracking, GPS, and onboard data logging.

Before deployment, the robot records its starting GPS coordinates at the entry point of the pipeline. Once inside, GPS is no longer available, so the robot relies on its IMU to calculate traveled distance, orientation, and relative position.

A differential pressure sensor continuously monitors pressure patterns. Any abnormal pressure drop is analyzed to determine the presence of a leak. At the exact moment a leak is detected, the robot correlates the pressure anomaly with its internally calculated position to determine the leak’s location relative to the entry point.

Introduction

Water Distribution Networks

Water distribution networks are among the most critical components of urban infrastructure. They ensure the continuous supply of clean water to homes, industries, and public facilities.

However, underground main pipelines and distribution lines are highly vulnerable to leaks caused by corrosion, pressure fluctuations, aging pipes, and mechanical damage.

The Real Problem

  • Water loss and waste of treated water.
  • Soil erosion and infrastructure damage.
  • Pipe bursts and increased maintenance costs.
  • Higher energy consumption.
  • Large financial losses for utility companies.

Methodology

Structure

The robot’s main body is a cylinder with pointed ends designed to reduce friction and move smoothly inside pipelines. Flexible joints allow bending and adaptation to pipe curves.

Electronic System

  • Arduino Uno R3 as the main processing unit.
  • Micro-SD module for data logging with timestamps.
  • Rechargeable 9–12V battery with a 5V regulator.

Mechanical & Navigation System

Motors rotate clockwise and counterclockwise to move forward, backward, left, and right. Servo motors control the joints to provide flexibility and adaptability.

Sensing System

  • GPS records coordinates before entering the pipeline.
  • IMU calculates distance traveled and monitors movement.
  • Differential pressure sensor detects pressure changes.
  • SD module stores data for later analysis.

Software Logic

How the Arduino Software Works

The Arduino software continuously reads all sensors, detects pressure drops, and computes leak location using IMU displacement added to the GPS starting coordinates.

Key events such as “leak detected,” pressure values, distance, heading, and calculated coordinates are stored on the SD card. 

Read GPS start point
Loop:
  Read pressure sensor
  Read IMU displacement
  Calculate current position
  If pressure drop detected:
      Mark leak detected
      Save pressure, distance, heading
      Save calculated coordinates
Store data on SD card

Background Research

MIT PipeGuard Robot

PipeGuard is a soft in-pipe robot that moves with water flow. It has a flexible rubber skirt that touches pipe walls. When there is a leak, escaping water creates a small pressure drop, which the robot senses while passing next to the leak.

Disadvantages
  • Relies on water flow to move.
  • Works mainly in smooth, constant-diameter pipes.
  • Prototype cost is more than $200.
  • Not commercially available for real field use.

Xylem SmartBall

SmartBall is a free-swimming device inserted into water pipelines. It drifts with the water flow while acoustic, pressure, and flow sensors record anomalies that may indicate leaks.

Disadvantages
  • No control over its movement.
  • Depends entirely on water flow.
  • Needs a human operator to catch it at another point.
  • Time-consuming for long or complex pipelines.
  • Branching networks cannot be examined effectively.

My Solution

Our project presents a low-cost leak detection robot under $100 that combines active navigation, accurate leak localization, and compatibility with most water pipe types.

Discussion

IMU – MPU6050

Chosen for its compact size, low cost, and reliable motion/orientation tracking for accurate navigation.

MPXV7002DP Pressure Sensor

Selected for its high sensitivity to small pressure changes, enabling precise leak detection and easy integration with Arduino.

Expected Results

Accurately detect small pressure drops using the MPXV7002DP, allowing identification of leaks as small as a few millibars.

Track movement and estimate distance inside pipes using the MPU6050 IMU, enabling precise leak localization with an expected error margin less than 5–10%.

Navigate through different pipe diameters and bends using the active mobility system while maintaining stability.

Achieve low-cost performance under $100 while matching or exceeding the accuracy of higher-cost commercial robots.

Future Plans

Automated Repair Module

Upgrade the robot into a full leak-management system by adding an automated repair module capable of injecting fast-curing sealing materials at the leak point.

Eliminating GPS Module

Try to eliminate the NEO-6M GPS module to reduce overall cost and rely entirely on accelerometer readings from the IMU for distance estimation.

Advanced Signal Filtering

Improve software processing and signal filtering to maintain accurate tracking without GPS support.
